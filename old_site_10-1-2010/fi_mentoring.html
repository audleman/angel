WHY MENTORING

  • Refine your perception skills
  • Enhance your understanding of movement patterns
  • Develop intuitive intelligence
  • Recognize valuable nonverbal clues
  • Reduce inquiry time before and during a lesson
  • Move away from "generic" FI lessons -making them unique and exclusive
  • Learn varied ways to address common issues
  • Improve your abilities to give effective lessons
  • Find ways to better reach and engage your clients
  • Link Functional Integration to activities of daily living
  • Work with human rapport issues that can add or detract from a lesson
  • Maintain the pleasure and excitement in your work
  • Increase your level of experience as a practitioner
  • Discuss strategies that can enhance business and professional practice
  • Learn to work in partnership with colleagues

MENTORING FOR TRAINEES

Like many training students who have participated in our mentoring supervision you can enhance your learning process.

  • It helps you keep your momentum, which can often get lost between segments. The next segment doesn't feel like a "cold start".
  • It gives you more confidence and competence in starting up ATM classes.
  • It stimulates continuous learning.
  • It helps you integrate the method in your lives.

MENTORING FOR PRACTITIONERS

This mentoring is a tailor made advanced training open to recent grads as well as experienced practitioners. Deepen your understanding of fundamental lessons or learn something entirely new and unfamiliar!

Note: applies to Continuing Education requirements for FGNA certification renewal.

MENTORING FORMATS

  1. Two practitioners come for a lesson
    a) Angel or Richard gives one practitioner a lesson while the other observes.
    b) The thinking behind the FI process is discussed before, during and/or after the lesson.
    c) The practitioner who received the FI gives the lesson to their colleague while being mentored.
  2. Bring a friend or a client, give them a supervised lesson and receive feedback.
  3. Bring a guest. Angel or Richard gives them a lesson while you observe. We discuss the FI afterwards (guests either have the option to remain for the discussion or leave.
  4. Give Angel or Richard an FI. We will offer feedback during and/or after the session.
  5. If you are a practitioner, bring a client for whom additional input would be welcomed. Angel or Richard would interview and give them an FI while you observe. We then discuss the lesson afterwards.

Of course, other formats can be created to meet your individual needs. We also offer supervision to groups of trainees or practitioners.


MENTORING FOR OUT OF TOWNERS

For those who come from out of town, Angel and Richard suggest weekend workshop intensives. If you need a place to stay, there are many reasonable hotels nearby.

HOW LONG IS A MENTORING SESSION?

Each session last an hour and a half to two hours (depending on how many people attend).
